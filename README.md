OVERVIEW
This project analyzes Lassa fever outbreak data from 2020-2025 using Power BI to explore trends in confirmed cases, deaths, and seasonality patterns. The goal is to generate insights that support public health decision-making and outbreak preparedness.

OBJECTIVES
Analyze yearly trends of confirmed cases and deaths
Identify seasonal patterns of outbreaks
Compare suspected vs confirmed cases
Provide insights for disease monitoring

TOOLS USED
Power BI
Microsoft Excel / CSV dataset
Data cleaning and transformation

DATA CLEANING / PREPARATION
Standardized date formats
Standardized data types

1. High-Level Metrics (Total Cases, Total Death, Case Fatality Rate, Peak Week Cases)
Over the evaluated five-year period, the dataset tracks a cumulative baseline of 6,456 Total Cases and 1,008 Total Deaths. This results in a Case Fatality Rate of 15.6%, meaning approximately one out of every six individuals with a recorded case succumbed to the illness. The system's maximum operational stress occurred during the absolute worst transmission spike, hitting a threshold of 137 Peak Week Cases.

2. Weekly Trend and Death Trend
The Weekly Trend and Death Trend line charts reveal a highly synchronized, predictable annual rhythm. Every year, without fail, case volumes and mortality sharply escalate at the exact same time, showing that the intensity of the outbreak directly drives the toll on human life. While 2020 saw a sharp, severe initial spike, 2021 showed remarkable suppression across both trends before the data began climbing again in subsequent years.

3. Annual Epidemiological Trends
The Annual Epidemiological Trends bar chart isolates the total volume handled each year, illustrating a clear pattern of suppression and resurgence. After the initial 2020 outbreak, cases dropped drastically in 2021, marking it as the lowest year in the dataset. However, control was short-lived; volumes climbed steadily through 2022 and 2023, ultimately peaking in 2024 as the highest volume year on record before seeing a slight ease in 2025.

4. Seasonal Analysis
The Seasonal Analysis area chart breaks down the timeline by quarters to show exactly when the disease moves fastest. Transmission is heavily front-loaded, with an acute concentration of cases occurring entirely within Quarter 1. Once this initial wave clears, volumes drop sharply into Quarter 2, reach a stable plateau during Quarter 3, and begin a gradual upward crawl in Quarter 4 as the next annual cycle builds.

5. Disease Case Confirmation Status
The Disease Case Confirmation Status stacked bar chart highlights a significant operational gap between frontline tracking and laboratory capacity. Across all six years, the dark green bars showing the Sum of Suspected Cases completely dominate the visual volume. In contrast, the light grey slivers representing the Sum of Confirmed Cases make up only a small fraction of the total, pointing to a major bottleneck in testing and official verification workflows.
